RESPONSIVE DESIGNS

DEFAULT PAGES

The default pages on our sites are a big selling point to our clients, due to the fact that they are often a big branding exercise and serve as the main "face" of their sites. Because of this, most of the designs are very bespoke, which makes designing them responsively a fun challenge: preserving the client's branding and identity guidelines while maintaining our platforms functionality on all screen sizes.

PRODUCT PAGES

Since the majority of our users enter into our sites through product pages, our product page has continually evolved. Historically, along side any evolutions we designed for desktop, we also ported the changes to mobile as well. However, as our mobile user base has grown, we have more recently started designing for mobile and scaling up to desktop, utilizing a more mobile-first mentality.

UTILITY BAR: DEALERS

Similar to how we templatized our non-program sites, the revamped navigation bar, dubbed the "utility bar", was an effort to standardize parts of the master page so that responsive design was better, more consistent, and easier to manage. Rather than maintaining 70+ semi-responsive master page template styles, the new design provides a single versatile yet consistent module that can be used across our sites to improve functionality and simplify responsive designs.

UTILITY BAR: OEMS

In addition to applying to dealer sites (which necessitated dealer contact information, a cart widget, etc.), I also designed a slimmed-down version for our OEM sites that followed the same structure to provide further consistency, as well as utilize the same responsive code that was created for its dealer counterpart.
