ERGONOMIC HAND RAKE

RESEARCHING THE HAND

The goal of this project was to design and prototype an ergonomic garden tool, which in my case was a hand rake. To design a tool that comfortably fits the hand, I started by researching what makes the hand comfortable.

BENCHMARKING CURRENT PRODUCTS

To continue my research, I took what I learned about comfortable hand positions and compared them to products currently on the market. I found tools that had similar handles, and took note of how they made the hand feel.

IDEATING FORMS

I sat down and shelled out twenty different designs to create a range of forms that I could potentially pursue, keeping in mind what I learned from my research as well as my benchmarking of existing products.

NARROWING DOWN

After sifting though my preliminary designs, I eliminated forms that were uncomfortable, strenuous, and impractical. I narrowed the designs down to five potential forms and drew up orthographics to help get a better idea of each.

MOCKING UP FORMS

From there, I mocked up rough pink foam models of the five so I could actually see and feel them in 3D. I had people with various hand sizes hold each of the forms to get their opinions and help further rule out uncomfortable designs.

CHOOSING THE FORM

I chose the form shown above, as it had a clean design, was the most comfortable, and got the most positive feedback while testing. I took that design and fine-tuned the dimensions, then made final orthographics to get ready for construction.

THE ROUGH MODEL

I made a rough model of the final form so that I could confirm that the dimensions were correct and everything was indeed comfortable. It also gave me another opportunity to work out any kinks and small details in the form.

CONSTRUCTING THE FINAL MODEL

I used sign foam and clear acrylic rod to prototype the form. I sawed, filed and sanded the foam down to shape, and used a heat gun to drape the rods onto a curved mold. From there I painted the model and added finishing touches.

THE ERGONOMIC HAND RAKE

The final model finished quite well, and I was happy with how polished and sturdy it ended
up being. In the final review, it received very positive feedback from both classmates and external reviewers. The handle fit almost every hand it was held by, and people liked not only how it felt, but also how it looked.
