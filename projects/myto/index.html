MYTO RING

INITIAL CONCEPTS

This project served as in introduction to 3D printing to fit the body, and its end goal was to 3D print a ring out of metal. To begin, we were to come up with ten different deigns, focusing on those that could take advantage of this medium of production.

REFINING THE PATTERN

Although I was pleased with many of the forms, the “mitochondria” ring best illustrated a form that would be very hard to make with any method other than 3D printing, so I decided to pursue that one. I started ideating the proportions of the cross-section to give me a starting point for a 3D model.

CREATING THE MODEL

For each new cross-section I created, I extruded in SolidWorks so that I could visualize the form in 3D, and then printed a model to see how it looked on the finger. I played with different heights, widths, and diameters to see which dimensions worked best as a ring.

FINISHING THE MODEL

After perfecting the dimensions and adding some other aesthetic changes, finally I arrived at an ideal form for which I made one last refined model. I then sent the ring off to Shapeways to be 3D printed in stainless steel.

RENDERING & CREATING THE FAMILY

While the ring was being printed, I focused on the branding of the ring, and thus the MYTO brand was born. I liked a few of the other ideations of MYTO that I had created along the way, so I decided to create a family of rings that varied slightly, but had the same mitochondria feel to them.

THE POSTER

Once I got the branding finished and the ring back and photographed, I created a brand poster to show the personality of the product line. The poster showcases the ring that I got printed, as well as renderings of all the rings that would be available.

MYTO RING

The ring recieved postitve feedback on the design, however the print quality of the metal was a bit grittier than I had expected. Another option from Shapeways involved the ring being 3D printed out of wax and then cast in metal, which would have produced a smoother result. However, I like having something that was actually 3D printed in metal, and was happy
with the result.
