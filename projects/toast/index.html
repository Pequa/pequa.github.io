TOAST


The objective for this project was to create a personal or social product that enhanced the wine drinking experience. The personal product would enhance the individual experience through memory making and/or skill building, and the social product would enhance the experience though engagement with others and real-time sharing.

AN INDUVIDUAL & SOCIAL SOLUTION


The project problem was to create either a social product or a personal product that enhanced the wine drinking experience. But after ideating some potential solutions, I realized that the beauty of an app is that it can cover both of those markets. So I began thinking of what kind of functions an app could have so that it could successfully serve as personal information app as well as a social media app. What resulted was Toast.

THE WINE CHARM


For the social aspect of the app, I didn’t want to limit the app to social media, I wanted to encourage real-world interaction. I had the idea that in social environments, toasting wine glasses is a common practice. In addition, wine charms are also very common in social environments for differentiating wine and expressing individual style.

So what I decided to base the app off of is this: if everyone were to have a "smart" wine charm that could sense when its parent wine glass was being toasted, the charms would be able to talk to each other and record who toasted with who, when, and where. Then, once two glasses were “toasted”, two options would be available: share the toast with friends (socially), or keep add the toast to your collection (personally).

THE SOCIAL EXPERIENCE


Once the toasts were logged, if you chose to share the toast friends, the app would take the information gathered from the toast and translate it into a Facebook status. This would give you the opportunity to share the experience that you’re having with friends, telling them what you are doing, where, and with whom.

THE PERSONAL EXPERIENCE


Say, however, you did not want to share the event with friends, but you wanted to remember what kind of wine you are drinking and how you liked it. Then, you could use Toast to take a picture of the label, from which it would log the wine name, vintage year, and time you had it. The app would then prompt you for a rating, and log that along with the wine information to create a unique, personal wine history.

DATA IS BEAUTIFUL


With all the information about the wine stored, as well as your opinion on it, the app would then able to show you data about your preferences on wine, and then make recommendations on other wines you might like. This information would help you become in tune to your preferences, which would help guide you towards wine that is better suited for you.

TOAST


The app received positive feedback at the final presentation of the mini-project, including positive feedback from a liquor store owner and wine enthusiast, who said that an app like this would definitely be appreciated in the wine drinking community. I was also very pleased with how it turned out, and would love to take Toast and actually build and produce it one day.
