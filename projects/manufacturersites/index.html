A SAMPLE OF MY WORK

Rather than recruiting individual dealerships, our main focus is partnering with manufacturers, who then give incentives for their dealerships to sign up with us. The typical format we use for a manufacturer is a national parts site where you can browse parts and see nearby dealerships, and from there select an individual dealership, which transfers you to their own site to buy parts. BEFORE: MAKING A CHANGE

Our old sites (left) were very utilitarian and focused more on functionality rather than marketing, and did not do a good job of creating a retail experience for the brand like the manufacturer’s own site did (right), nor did they match the brand guidelines set by the manufacturers they were for. When our development team began porting all of our sites over to an updated platform, I designed a new front end for all of our manufacturers, as well as some functionality updates to improve the usability of the sites.

UPDATING FUNCTIONALITY: NAVIGATION & SEO

On the old platform, navigation was handled by a series of dropdowns that you would use to select vehicle fitment. A disadvantage to this format was that all steps were chosen before information on the page updated, which was not optimal for search engines. Now, with individual buttons for navigation, “steps” are created along the way that serve as landing pages for a range of search queries (there is now a landing page for someone searching for Subaru WRX parts, as well as 2017 Subaru WRX parts, etc.), which has increased SEO value.

UPDATING FUNCTIONALITY: MERCHANDISING PRODUCTS

As mentioned above, our old sites were quite utilitarian and did not do a great job of merchandising products, especially for apparel and accessories and the lifestyle that came along with them. I took this into account when re-designing the sites, using larger pictures and more open layouts that created a more retail look and feel compared to our previous sites.

UPDATING FUNCTIONALITY: PRODUCTS & ASSEMBLIES

The “interior” product and assembly pages on our sites got a facelift as well when re-designing for the new platform. I re-styled the pages (while keeping layout more or less the same), and helped update a few aspects of the page in order to make user experience and navigation between products and assemblies better.

UPDATING FUNCTIONALITY: WCAG

One of the things we changed when moving our sites over to our new platform was making our sites AA compliant with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines, which aim to make web sites more accessible. This involves designing so that the pages are responsive, color contrast is sufficient, font sizes are large enough, organizing things in a way that screen readers can tab through them, and more. I had to keep all of this in mind when re-designing our sites.
